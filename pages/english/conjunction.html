English conjunction for Italian language speaking



Coordinating Conjunctions: This type of conjunction is easy to remember and these conjunctions join the words, phrases as well as independent clauses.
Subordinating Conjunctions: This type of conjunction is used with a dependent clause. Subordinating Conjunctions are often used at the starting of the sentences.


For → explains reason

  •  I want to collect sample question papers, for a constructive revision.

And → add things or sentences

  •  I want to collect sample question papers and old test papers, for a constructive revision.

Nor → represents an alternative negative idea

  • I don’t like to practice worksheets nor model papers before the exams.

But → represents a contrast

  • Practicing sample questions is good for the students, but it is important during the test preparation.

Or  → presents a choice

  • As time is limited, hence, students can practice guess papers or old test papers.

Yet → represents a contrasting idea that refers the preceding idea

  • I want to read for a long time, yet I could not sit for more than 2 hours.

So → refers result, effect or consequences

  • I want to score well in the final exam, so I practice model papers and other study documents rigorously.



As → at the same time as; while; when

  • We all waved goodbye to Liz as she drove away. (We waved and she drove away at the same time)

  • I watched her as she opened the letter.

  • As I walked along the street, I looked in the shop windows.

  • Can you turn off the light as you go out, please?

  • Kate slipped as she was getting off the bus.

  • We met Paul as we were leaving the hotel.

  • As the day went on, the weather got worse. (Two things happening together in a longer period of time)

  • I began to enjoy the job more as I got used to it.

Just as → exactly at that moment

  • Just as I sat down, the phone rang.

  • I had to leave just as the conversation was getting interesting.

As → Because

  • As I was hungry, I decided to find somewhere to eat.

  • As it was a public holiday last Thursday, most of the shops were shut.
